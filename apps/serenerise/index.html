Frequently asked questions

What is Brahma Muhurta?

Brahma Muhurta is the sacred time window beginning roughly 1 hour 36 minutes before sunrise and lasting about 48 minutes. Classical Vedic texts describe it as the most auspicious time for meditation, study, and spiritual practice — the mind is naturally calm, and sattva (clarity) is said to be at its peak.

Why does the time change every day?

Because it's tied to sunrise, which shifts slightly each day with the earth's orbit and your latitude. SereneRise recalculates automatically — you never need to look it up.

Does the app need internet?

No. Once installed, all calculations happen on your device. The app works entirely offline.

Why does it need location permission?

Sunrise times depend on your latitude and longitude, so the app needs to know where you are to calculate the correct window. Your location is used only for this calculation — it is never transmitted or stored on a server. See the privacy policy for details.

The widget isn't updating. What should I do?

Android aggressively manages background processes, especially on certain manufacturer skins. If the widget stops refreshing, open the app once — it will force an update. Also check that battery optimization isn't restricting SereneRise.

Does it work in the Southern Hemisphere?

Yes. The sunrise calculation is purely astronomical and works correctly anywhere on Earth, including high latitudes where sunrise times shift dramatically through the year.

Is there an iOS version?

Not yet. SereneRise is currently Android-only.
