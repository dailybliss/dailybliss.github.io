Frequently asked questions

What is Brahma Muhurta?

Brahma Muhurta is the sacred time window beginning roughly 1 hour 36 minutes before sunrise and lasting about 48 minutes. Classical Vedic texts describe it as the most auspicious time for meditation, study, and spiritual practice.

How is this different from SereneRise?

SereneAlarm includes everything in SereneRise — precise Brahma Muhurta times, home screen widget, automatic hourly refresh — plus a built-in alarm that automatically wakes you at the right time each morning. No need to manually set or adjust your alarm.

Does the alarm adjust automatically?

Yes. Since Brahma Muhurta shifts with the sunrise throughout the year, the alarm recalculates every day. You set it once and it stays correct — even as sunrise changes by over an hour between seasons.

Why does the time change every day?

Because it's tied to sunrise, which shifts slightly each day with the earth's orbit and your latitude. SereneAlarm recalculates automatically — you never need to look it up.

Does the app need internet?

No. Once installed, all calculations happen on your device. The app works entirely offline.

Why does it need location permission?

Sunrise times depend on your latitude and longitude, so the app needs to know where you are to calculate the correct window and set the alarm at the right time. Your location is never transmitted or stored on a server. See the privacy policy for details.

Can I choose the alarm sound?

Yes. The app includes several calming alarm tones designed for a gentle wake-up. You can also set the alarm to vibrate only.

The widget isn't updating. What should I do?

Android aggressively manages background processes, especially on certain manufacturer skins. If the widget stops refreshing, open the app once — it will force an update. Also check that battery optimization isn't restricting SereneAlarm.

Does it work in the Southern Hemisphere?

Yes. The sunrise calculation is purely astronomical and works correctly anywhere on Earth.

Is there an iOS version?

Not yet. SereneAlarm is currently Android-only.
